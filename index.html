Understanding Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

A comprehensive guide based on information from leading health organizations.

Overview

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common hormonal disorder affecting women of reproductive age. It is characterized by irregular menstrual cycles, elevated levels of male hormones (androgens), and polycystic ovaries. PCOS can lead to various health complications, including infertility, metabolic issues, and psychological challenges. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Symptoms

  • Irregular or absent menstrual periods
  • Excess facial or body hair (hirsutism)
  • Acne and oily skin
  • Weight gain or difficulty losing weight
  • Thinning hair or hair loss on the scalp
  • Darkening of skin, particularly along neck creases, in the groin, and underneath breasts
  • Skin tags in the armpits or neck area
  • Difficulty with fertility

Causes

The exact cause of PCOS is unknown; however, several factors may contribute to its development:

  • Genetics: PCOS often runs in families, suggesting a hereditary component.
  • Insulin resistance: Many women with PCOS have insulin resistance, leading to higher insulin levels, which may increase androgen production.
  • Inflammation: Low-grade inflammation may stimulate polycystic ovaries to produce androgens.

:contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Diagnosis

There is no single test to definitively diagnose PCOS. Healthcare providers typically diagnose PCOS if at least two of the following are present:

  • Irregular or absent menstrual periods
  • Elevated levels of androgens
  • Polycystic ovaries visible on ultrasound

Additional tests may include blood tests to measure hormone levels and glucose tolerance, as well as pelvic exams and ultrasounds. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Treatment

While there is no cure for PCOS, various treatments can help manage symptoms:

  • Lifestyle changes: Weight loss through diet and exercise can improve insulin sensitivity and regulate menstrual cycles.
  • Medications: Hormonal birth control can regulate periods and reduce androgen levels. Metformin may improve insulin resistance.
  • Fertility treatments: Clomiphene citrate or letrozole can stimulate ovulation for women trying to conceive.
  • Hair removal treatments: Laser hair removal or electrolysis can address excess hair growth.
